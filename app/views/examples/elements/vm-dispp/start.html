Tell DVLA you've sold or bought a vehicle

Bought a vehicle into the trade

You must make sure that:

  • the name and address on the V5C registration certificate (logbook) are correct
  • any personalised registration numbers have been kept or transferred

You’ll need:

  • the 11 digit reference number from the latest V5C registration certificate (logbook)
  • consent of the seller to act on their behalf
Destroy the original V5C/3 (yellow slip) when you’ve completed the service. You don’t need to send it to DVLA.

You can’t use this service to keep or assign a vehicle registration number

When you can’t use this service

You can’t use this service to take the registration number off a vehicle.

What happens next

The vehicle’s registered keeper will be removed from DVLA records. They’ll be sent confirmation by post.

DVLA will automatically refund any vehicle tax due to the address on the V5C and cancel any direct debit.

Other ways to apply

If you can’t use the online service, you’ll need to:

  • complete V5C/3 with your trader details
  • sign the V5C (along with the seller)
  • send the V5C/3 to DVLA
